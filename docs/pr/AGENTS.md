### What "Blended Learning" Means

**IMPORTANT:** In reading instruction, "blended learning" means **physical materials + digital platform** (like RAZ-Kids). It does NOT mean "AI instruction + human teacher."

**Our Blended Learning model (5 periods/week):**
| Periods | Activity | Materials | Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|
| 3/week | Teacher-led workbook class | Teacher projects article from app; students complete workbook exercises | Scripted lessons, QA/QC, metacognition training |
| 2/week | Extensive reading | App only | Research-based (Aka 2019) |

### Workbook Value Proposition

The workbooks serve three purposes:
1. **Curriculum Alignment** — Replaces whatever textbooks schools already use. No parallel curricula to manage. Everything integrates with the app.
2. **QA/QC via Scripted Lessons** — The "Big 4" protocol lives here. Consistent instruction regardless of individual teacher ability.
3. **Metacognition Training** — Teacher-led sessions explicitly teach students how to use the app effectively and think about their own learning.

The workbooks don't need to prove they're more effective than extensive reading alone—they replace other textbooks that also aren't research-validated. The added value is integration + quality control + explicit app usage instruction.

- **Workbooks:** 15 levels developed (A1-C1), printing being finalized, samples available late January 2026

## Evidence-Based Messaging

### The Aka 2019 Standard

**CRITICAL: All outcome claims must use this research-based language. Do NOT use "40% improvement" or similar percentage claims.**

**Approved evidence language:**
> A controlled one-year study (Aka, 2019) found that extensive reading instruction (2 hours/week) outperformed grammar instruction on reading test scores. On the post-test reading section (0-100 scale), the extensive reading group scored higher (61.78 vs 52.29; ~+9.5 points).

**How to apply:**
- "Evidence-backed reading gains (based on controlled extensive reading research)"
- "Research shows extensive reading outperforms traditional grammar instruction"
- "Measurable improvement validated by peer-reviewed research"
- Report school-specific outcomes transparently (pre/post deltas, reading volume, fidelity scores)

**What NOT to say:**
- "40% improvement" or any specific percentage
- "Guaranteed results"
- Outcome promises not tied to specific research

## Document Formats and Standards

### Markdown Structure
- Use clear heading hierarchy (H1 for title, H2 for major sections, H3 for subsections)
- Include horizontal rules (`---`) between major sections for visual separation
- Use bulleted lists for features/benefits, numbered lists for sequential steps
- Include tables for pricing, metrics, or comparative information
- Use blockquotes or callout formatting for critical warnings or notes

### Checklist Format
For implementation documents:
- Use `- [ ]` for uncompleted tasks
- Use `- [x]` for completed tasks (when updating status)
- Group related tasks under clear headings
- Assign priority levels (Critical, High, Medium, Low)
- Include estimated timelines
- Add verification checklist before finalization

### Strategic Document Format
For planning documents (like marketing-foundation-document.md):
- Start with Executive Summary
- Include Table of Contents for documents >10 pages
- Separate "Current State" from "Future Vision"
- Provide specific, measurable success metrics
- Include implementation roadmap with phases/timeline
- Add budget allocation guidance
- Conclude with approval process and next steps

## Key Strategic Principles

### Credibility First
All new materials must:
- Be radically honest about current scale
- Focus on technology innovation and research-backed outcomes (Aka 2019)
- Position as innovative partner, not established market leader
- Use case studies and specific results over broad claims

### Product-Market Fit
Strong alignment between products and target market needs:
- **Problem:** Private Thai schools can't recruit/retain foreign English teachers; need complete curriculum
- **Solution:** Blended learning (workbooks + app) with scripted lessons requiring minimal teacher training
- **Outcome:** Consistent, quality English instruction at scale without foreign teacher dependency

### Technology as Differentiator
The platform's competitive advantage is advanced AI (Google Gemini + GPT-5):
- Only extensive reading platform using both leading AI models
- Real-time adaptation to student performance
- Comprehensive CEFR coverage (Pre-A1 to C1)
- Spaced Repetition System for vocabulary retention

### Teacher Empowerment, Not Replacement
Critical messaging: AI enhances teachers, doesn't replace them:
- Blended model: Scripted workbook lessons + independent app reading
- Teachers become facilitators using scripted lesson plans
- Minimal training required (2-day onboarding)
- Teachers explicitly teach app usage and metacognitive strategies during workbook sessions
- Professional development opportunity for Thai teachers

## Important Terminology

- **CEFR:** Common European Framework of Reference for Languages (Pre-A1 through C2 levels)
- **Extensive Reading:** Research-based methodology (Aka 2019) focusing on reading volume and comprehension over intensive grammar study
- **Blended Learning:** Physical workbooks + digital app (NOT "AI + human teacher"); similar to RAZ-Kids model
- **App-Only:** Extensive reading platform without workbooks (2 periods/week recommended)
- **Managed Service:** Full outsourced solution where we provide contractor teachers + platform + workbooks (launches May 2027)
- **15-Period-Per-Week English Programs:** Comprehensive English programs in Thai private schools (typically 3 periods per day)
- **SRS:** Spaced Repetition System for vocabulary retention
- **EFL:** English as a Foreign Language
- **Big 4 Protocol:** QA framework (Uninterrupted Reading, Student Agency, Data-Driven Consensus, Workbook-First Protocol)
