## SCHOOL ASSEMBLY PROGRAM

### What This Is

Thai private schools regularly invite companies to conduct 1-2 hour whole-school assemblies. Companies provide educational entertainment (songs, games, activities) and schools get free programming without planning or paying contractors. This is standard practice in Thailand for milk companies, cereal brands, and educational services.

**Our Version:** One-hour English reading assemblies for Primary and Secondary students that:
- Educate students about the value of reading in English
- Demonstrate engaging activities from our curriculum approach
- Build excitement about English learning
- Introduce our platform to teachers and administrators
- Provide "samples" (trial access, promotional materials)

### Assembly Program Types

| Program | Duration | Audience | Focus |
|---------|----------|----------|-------|
| **Primary Show** | 60 minutes | Grades 3-6 (ages 8-12) | Fun, songs, simple games, reading excitement |
| **Secondary Show** | 60 minutes | Grades 7-12 (ages 12-18) | Interactive, competitive, future-focused |

### OUR ASSEMBLY TEAM & ASSETS

#### Team Roles

| Role | Person | Responsibilities |
|------|--------|------------------|
| **Lead MC (Thai)** | Experienced Thai MC | Runs the show, gives instructions, manages energy, speaks Thai for clarity |
| **English Teacher Character** | Fluent English speaker | Reads passages, demonstrates pronunciation, explains English concepts |
| **Dolphin Mascot** | Hired extra in costume | Comedy sidekick, photo ops, dance partner, prize distribution |

**Team Dynamic:** The Thai MC leads and keeps energy high. The English speaker plays the "teacher" role during reading activities. The Dolphin provides comic relief, reacts to answers, celebrates winners, and "helps" students during the dance.

**Prize Strategy:** Everyone leaves with a pen (brand awareness). Notebooks go to game winners (creates excitement, competition). This ensures every student has a branded item to take home.

### GAME FORMATS

We use team and individual competitions based on app reading passages. This demonstrates our methodology while being entertaining.

#### Game Format 1: Family Feud Style
- Two teams compete
- Questions based on reading passage shown on screen
- Teams huddle, captain gives answer
- Points for correct answers
- Dolphin mascot reacts (celebrates correct, sad face for wrong)

#### Game Format 2: Team Competition / Individual Finals
- All students divided into teams (by class or random)
- Teams compete in rounds
- Top individuals from winning teams compete in finals
- Creates both team spirit and individual recognition

#### Game Format 3: Speed Reading Challenge
- Passage displayed on screen
- Timer visible
- First team to answer comprehension question correctly wins
- Demonstrates how extensive reading improves speed

#### Game Format 4: Vocabulary Showdown
- Word appears on screen
- Teams race to give correct meaning or use in sentence
- Can include Thai-English translation rounds
- Dolphin "helps" struggling teams (comedy)

### PRIMARY ASSEMBLY PROGRAM (Grades 3-6)

#### Program Structure (60 minutes total)

| Time | Segment | Who Leads | Description |
|------|---------|-----------|-------------|
| 0-5 min | **Grand Entrance** | Thai MC + Dolphin | Jingle plays, Dolphin dances in, MC hypes crowd |
| 5-10 min | **Introduction** | Thai MC + English Speaker | Introduce team, explain what we'll do today |
| 10-15 min | **"Why Reading?" Story** | English Speaker | Story of "Nong Dolphin" who learned English through reading |
| 15-25 min | **Vocabulary Showdown** | Thai MC | Team competition, Dolphin "helps" struggling teams |
| 25-35 min | **Speed Reading Game** | Thai MC + English Speaker | Passage on screen, teams race to answer |
| 35-45 min | **Learn the Dolphin Dance** | Thai MC + Dolphin | Teach dance moves, practice together, final performance |
| 45-55 min | **Champion Round + Prizes** | Thai MC | Finals between top teams, notebook prizes for winners |
| 55-60 min | **Pen Distribution + Close** | All three | Everyone gets a pen, parent flyers, trial cards, photo ops |

#### Segment Details - Primary

**Grand Entrance (0-5 min):**
- Jingle plays on speakers
- Dolphin dances down the aisle, high-fiving students
- MC runs in: "สวัสดีครับ/ค่ะ! ใครพร้อมสนุกกับภาษาอังกฤษวันนี้บ้าง?!"
- Get students to make noise, raise hands
- Dolphin does silly poses

**"Why Reading?" Story (10-15 min):**
English Speaker tells the story of "Nong Dolphin" (นองโลมา):
- Couldn't understand English movies at age 8
- Started reading English 15 minutes per day
- After one year, could understand cartoons without subtitles
- Now loves English books and YouTube

Key message: "อ่านทุกวัน ภาษาอังกฤษง่ายขึ้นเรื่อยๆ!" (Read every day, English gets easier and easier!)

Dolphin acts out the story (sad at first, then happy, then celebrating)

**Vocabulary Showdown (15-25 min):**
- Divide into 4-6 teams (by classroom section)
- Word appears on screen with image
- Teams race to shout the English word
- Dolphin runs to winning team, celebrates with them
- Dolphin "whispers" hints to struggling teams (comedy)
- 10-15 words total

**Speed Reading Game (25-35 min):**
- Short passage displayed (age-appropriate)
- English Speaker reads aloud (modeling pronunciation)
- Question appears
- Teams discuss, captains answer
- Points tracked on screen
- 3-4 passages total

**Learn the Dolphin Dance (35-45 min):**
- MC announces: "เวลาเต้นกับน้องโลมา!"
- Jingle plays
- Dolphin demonstrates moves slowly
- Students stand up
- Teach 4-6 simple moves
- Practice with music
- Final full performance together
- Dolphin picks "best dancers" from crowd

**Champion Round (45-55 min):**
- Top 2 teams from earlier games compete in finals
- 3 quick questions
- Winning team members each get a notebook
- Photo with Dolphin

**Pen Distribution & Close (55-60 min):**
- Teachers help distribute pens to all students
- Parent flyers and trial cards handed out
- MC thanks school
- Dolphin available for photos
- Team collects teacher contact info

### SECONDARY ASSEMBLY PROGRAM (Grades 7-12)

#### Program Structure (60 minutes total)

| Time | Segment | Who Leads | Description |
|------|---------|-----------|-------------|
| 0-5 min | **Entrance + Hook** | Thai MC + Dolphin | Jingle plays, Dolphin enters (more "cool" than silly), MC opens with future hook |
| 5-12 min | **"Your Future" Talk** | English Speaker | English + careers, university, global opportunities |
| 12-25 min | **Family Feud Round** | Thai MC | Class vs class, reading passage questions |
| 25-38 min | **Speed Reading Championship** | Thai MC + English Speaker | Timed reading challenges, individual finalists |
| 38-48 min | **Champion Finals + Prizes** | Thai MC | Top individuals compete, notebook prizes |
| 48-55 min | **The Dolphin Dance Challenge** | Thai MC + Dolphin | Fun finale, but framed as "challenge" not just cute |
| 55-60 min | **Pen Distribution + Close** | All three | Everyone gets pen, trial cards, photo ops |

#### Segment Details - Secondary

**Note:** For secondary students, the Dolphin plays a "cooler" character - more like a friend/competitor than a cute mascot. Comedy comes from the Dolphin being overly confident, then "losing" to students.

**Entrance + Hook (0-5 min):**
- Jingle plays
- Dolphin walks in confidently (not silly dancing)
- MC: "ใครอยากทำงานที่ต่างประเทศบ้าง? ใครอยากเข้ามหาวิทยาลัยดีๆ?"
- Quick poll: raise hands for career dreams
- MC: "ทุกอย่างที่คุณอยากทำ... ต้องใช้ภาษาอังกฤษ"

**"Your Future" Talk (5-12 min):**
English Speaker presents (with Thai MC translating key points):

Key statistics:
- "Research shows extensive reading improves test scores by 9+ points" (Aka 2019)
- "Top Thai universities require English interviews"
- "International companies in Thailand pay 2-3x more for English speakers"
- "Scholarships, exchange programs, global careers - all require reading fluency"

Show quick examples:
- Thai YouTube stars who read English to learn content creation
- Thai entrepreneurs who expanded globally with English
- Exchange program opportunities

Key message: "Reading is the fastest way to improve your English. 15 minutes a day changes your future."

**Family Feud Round (12-25 min):**
- Divide into teams (by classroom)
- Reading passage displayed
- English Speaker reads aloud
- Question appears: "Survey says... what is the main idea?"
- Team captains buzz in (raise hand)
- Points for correct answers
- Dolphin acts as "judge" (dramatically reveals if answer is correct)
- 3-4 passages, escalating difficulty

**Speed Reading Championship (25-38 min):**
- Individual competition now
- Passage appears with timer
- Students read silently
- First to raise hand and answer correctly wins the round
- Top 4 students advance to finals
- Dolphin "competes" against students (always loses - comedy)

**Champion Finals (38-48 min):**
- Final 4 students on stage
- 3 challenging passages
- Elimination format
- Winner gets notebook + photo with Dolphin
- Runners-up get notebooks too

**Dolphin Dance Challenge (48-55 min):**
- MC: "น้องโลมาท้าทายทุกคน!"
- Framed as competition, not just cute activity
- Dolphin demonstrates moves
- Students who do it best get recognized
- Everyone dances together
- Jingle plays
- Energy peaks before close

**Pen Distribution & Close (55-60 min):**
- Teachers help distribute pens
- Trial cards: "ลองฟรี 7 วัน - scan QR code"
- MC thanks school
- Photo opportunities
- Team collects interested teacher contacts

### ADDITIONAL ENHANCEMENT IDEAS

Consider adding these elements as you refine the program:

#### 1. Photo Booth Moment
- Set up a branded backdrop after the show
- Students take photos with Dolphin
- Encourage sharing on social media / LINE
- Creates organic word-of-mouth

#### 2. Teacher Participation Segment
- Invite 2-3 teachers on stage to compete against students
- Teachers usually lose (students love this!)
- Gets teachers emotionally invested in the program
- Creates rapport before follow-up demo visit

#### 3. "Reading Pledge" Card
- Simple card: "I promise to read English 15 minutes a day"
- Students sign it and keep it
- Creates commitment, conversation with parents
- Could include QR code to trial

#### 4. Parent Communication
- Send message through school LINE group after assembly
- "Your children participated in our English reading assembly today!"
- Include photos, trial access link
- Parents hear about it from two sources (child + official message)

#### 5. "Reading Hero" Certificate
- Top performers get certificates
- "Reading Hero - [School Name] - January 2026"
- Students display at home (parent visibility)
- School logo + our logo (co-branding)

#### 6. Dolphin Catchphrases
Develop signature lines for the Dolphin:
- When students win: [Dolphin does celebration dance]
- When students lose: [Dolphin pretends to cry, then encourages]
- Before reading: [Dolphin puts on reading glasses]
- During dance: [Dolphin teaches signature move]

#### 7. School-Specific Customization
- Mention school name multiple times
- Reference local area or province
- Include school colors in slide design if possible
- Makes it feel exclusive, not generic

#### 8. "Challenge the Dolphin" Callback
- At the end: "Can your school beat [previous school]'s score?"
- Creates sense of competition across schools
- Gives reason to mention on social media
- Potential for regional "championships" later

#### 9. WhatsApp/LINE Sticker Pack
- Create Dolphin stickers
- Give out at assembly (QR code to download)
- Students use in daily messaging
- Ongoing brand visibility

### PRO TIPS FOR ASSEMBLY SUCCESS

#### Energy Management
- Start high energy and stay there
- If energy drops, have Dolphin do something silly
- Use music transitions to reset energy
- Never let a silence last more than 3 seconds

#### Timing Flexibility
- Have a "cut this if short on time" segment identified
- Have an "add this if extra time" game ready
- Schools often run behind schedule - adapt

#### Technical Backup Plan
- Always have phone backup of jingle
- Download videos locally (don't rely on internet)
- Bring portable speaker in case school PA fails
- Test everything 30 minutes before start

#### Working with Teachers
- Brief teachers before assembly on how they can help
- Ask them to manage their classroom sections during team games
- Invite English department head to say a few words (gets buy-in)
- Give teachers pens too (they're also brand ambassadors)

#### Managing Large Groups
- 300+ students: Split into Primary/Secondary shows
- 500+ students: May need two shows per level
- Use section/class divisions for team games (natural groupings)
- Enlist more teacher help for prize distribution

#### After Every Assembly: Debrief
Within 24 hours, team discusses:
- What worked well?
- What didn't land?
- Any technical issues?
- Student reactions to note?
- Teacher/admin feedback?

Update playbook based on learnings.

### TELEMARKETING FOR ASSEMBLY BOOKINGS

#### Why Hire a Telemarketer?

| Without Telemarketer | With Telemarketer |
|---------------------|-------------------|
| Core team makes 5-10 calls/day between other work | Dedicated caller makes 40-60 calls/day |
| Inconsistent outreach | Systematic coverage of target schools |
| MC/English speaker distracted from show prep | Team focuses on delivery, telemarketer on booking |
| ~20 assemblies/month maximum | 40-60+ assemblies/month possible |

**Cost:** 15,000-20,000 baht/month for full-time telemarketer
**ROI:** If telemarketer books 20 extra assemblies/month = 8,000 extra students reached = 400 baht/1,000 students

#### Telemarketer Job Requirements

**Must Have:**
- Native Thai speaker
- Clear, friendly phone voice
- Comfortable with rejection
- Basic computer skills (updating spreadsheet)
- Reliable, punctual

**Nice to Have:**
- Previous telemarketing or sales experience
- Basic English understanding
- Experience calling schools or institutions

**Training Required:**
- 2 hours on product/company overview
- 2 hours on script practice
- 1 hour on spreadsheet/tracking system
- Ongoing coaching first 2 weeks

## DEMO VISIT PROCESS

### What This Is

A demo visit is a 90-minute meeting at a school where we present the platform in detail to decision-makers (administrators, English department heads). This is different from assemblies - demos are sales meetings, not student activities.

### When to Request a Demo

- After a successful school assembly
- When a school contacts us directly (inquiry)
- When distributor partner identifies an interested school
- When referred by another school

### Demo Visit Checklist

#### Before the Visit (1 week prior)

**Gather Information:**
- [ ] School name, address, directions
- [ ] Number of students (by level: primary/secondary)
- [ ] Current English curriculum (textbooks, foreign teachers?)
- [ ] Decision-maker names and titles
- [ ] Any specific pain points mentioned

**Prepare Materials:**
- [ ] Laptop with presentation loaded
- [ ] Tablet with app installed (student account, demo content)
- [ ] Backup on USB drive
- [ ] Printed one-pagers (Blended Learning, pricing)
- [ ] Sample workbook (if available)
- [ ] Business cards
- [ ] Notepad for taking notes
- [ ] Contract/MOU template (just in case)

**Logistics:**
- [ ] Confirm meeting time and attendees
- [ ] Map route, plan to arrive 15 minutes early
- [ ] Charge all devices
- [ ] Dress professionally

#### During the Visit (90 minutes)

| Time | Activity | What to Do |
|------|----------|------------|
| 0-15 min | **Discovery** | Ask questions, understand their situation |
| 15-45 min | **Presentation** | Show platform, explain blended learning model |
| 45-60 min | **Demo** | Let them use the app, show teacher dashboard |
| 60-75 min | **Q&A** | Answer questions, address concerns |
| 75-90 min | **Next Steps** | Discuss pricing, timeline, pilot program |

#### Discovery Questions (Ask First, Present Second)

**About Their Current Situation:**
1. "How many periods of English do students have per week?"
2. "Do you have foreign English teachers? How many?"
3. "What textbooks are you currently using for English?"
4. "What are the biggest challenges with your English program?"
5. "How do you currently measure student progress?"

**About Their Needs:**
6. "What would an ideal English program look like for you?"
7. "Is improving English outcomes a priority for the school this year?"
8. "What has prevented you from making changes before?"

**About Their Process:**
9. "Who else would need to be involved in this decision?"
10. "What is your timeline for the new school year planning?"
11. "Do you have budget allocated for curriculum materials?"

**Take notes on their answers - you will need them for follow-up!**

#### Presentation Approach

**DO:**
- Connect everything to their specific situation
- Use their language (if they said "foreign teacher shortage," use that phrase)
- Show, don't just tell (demo the app)
- Acknowledge limitations honestly
- Focus on the Big 4 QA protocol as the differentiator

**DON'T:**
- Rush through slides
- Oversell or exaggerate
- Talk more than 50% of the time
- Ignore their questions
- Push for decision before they're ready

#### Handling Common Objections

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "It's too expensive" | "Let's compare: one foreign teacher costs 35,000+ baht/month for one classroom. Our Blended Learning model costs 2,200-2,400 baht/student/year and works for ALL classrooms. Would you like to see the ROI calculation?" |
| "Our teachers aren't tech-savvy" | "That's exactly why we designed the scripted workbook lessons. Teachers don't need to be tech experts - they follow the lesson plan, and students use the app independently. We also provide 2-day training." |
| "We tried e-learning before and it didn't work" | "What happened? [Listen] Our approach is different - we use blended learning with physical workbooks, not just screens. And our Big 4 QA protocol ensures consistent implementation." |
| "We need to think about it" | "Of course. What specific information would help you make a decision? [Pause] Would a pilot program with one class help you evaluate?" |
| "Can you give us a discount?" | "Our pricing is designed to be affordable for Thai schools. The standard rate is X. For schools implementing across multiple levels, we can discuss volume arrangements." |

#### After the Visit (Within 24 Hours)

**Send Follow-Up Email:**
- Thank them for their time
- Summarize key points discussed
- Answer any questions you couldn't answer during the meeting
- Attach any requested materials
- Propose specific next steps with dates

**Update CRM/Spreadsheet:**
- Meeting notes
- Interest level (Hot / Warm / Cold)
- Next action and date
- Decision-maker names and contacts

## LEAD MANAGEMENT

### Lead Stages

Every school contact moves through these stages:

| Stage | Definition | Next Action |
|-------|------------|-------------|
| **1. Suspect** | School in target list, not yet contacted | Make initial contact |
| **2. Lead** | School expressed interest or agreed to receive information | Send information, schedule call |
| **3. Qualified Lead** | School has budget, authority, need, and timeline | Schedule demo visit |
| **4. Opportunity** | Demo completed, actively considering | Follow up, address objections |
| **5. Proposal** | Formal proposal/quote sent | Negotiate, answer questions |
| **6. Closed Won** | Contract signed | Begin onboarding |
| **7. Closed Lost** | School decided not to proceed | Note reason, follow up next term |
